The Technology

Built from actual fan physics.

Blade-Pass Amplitude Modulation

Three asymmetric fan blades carve your vocal at 18–72 Hz. Each blade creates a fast-attack, slow-release gate as it crosses the vocal path. This is not smooth tremolo — it is a hard occlusion gate. The voice literally disappears between blade passes. The brain interpolates the missing pitch, creating the "Auto-Tune before Auto-Tune" illusion.

3 blades per rotation 18–72 Hz pass range 30 dB AM depth (GRILLE)

Housing Comb + Motor Stack

The plastic fan enclosure creates a 4–8 tap comb filter from sound reflections between the grille and rear housing (0.4–3.5 ms delays). The induction motor injects a 60 Hz fundamental + 120 Hz harmonic with bandpass-filtered sidebands. At BEHIND position, this is the dominant texture — warm, humming, plastic-box nostalgia.

4–8 comb taps 60 / 120 Hz motor hum BPF sideband modulation

Vintage Pitch Mode

Pitch correction that gates OFF during blade close. Instead of trying to pitch-correct silence, FanTune lets the chop speak. The grain shifter engages only during the open phase of each blade pass. The result: the "perceptual tune" that 2005 bedroom singers discovered — pitch correction that sounds like it's fighting through a fan, not applied on top of it.

YIN v2 pitch detection Scale-snap + glide + humanize Gates off during blade occlusion

FAQ

Common questions.

What does "NoDAW" mean?
NoDAW means you can use FanTune without opening a DAW at all. The browser version (demo.html) loads in any modern browser — no install, no account, no plugins. Just open the page, load audio, and hear the effect. Export WAV and upload to SoundCloud, TikTok, or your DAW for mixing. The VST3/AU/CLAP plugin version adds DAW integration for producers who want pitch correction, MIDI control, and preset management in their existing workflow.
Is this just another Auto-Tune clone?
No. FanTune is the opposite of Auto-Tune. The primary effect is amplitude modulation from physical fan blades — the voice gets literally chopped into pieces. Pitch correction is a secondary layer that gates off during blade close. The "pitchy" sound comes from the chop itself, not from grain shifting. This is what people actually heard when they sang into fans in 2005, not what modern pitch correction sounds like.
What DAWs are supported?
Any DAW that supports VST3 (Ableton Live 11+, FL Studio, Cubase, Reaper, Studio One, Bitwig) or AU (Logic Pro, GarageBand). CLAP format is supported for Bitwig and Reaper with CLAP support. The standalone version works as a regular desktop audio application with drag-and-drop file loading — no DAW needed.
Can I buy once and use on multiple machines?
Yes. The Pro license includes 2 activations — use on your studio desktop and your laptop. The Studio license includes 5 activations for larger setups. All licenses are perpetual with free v1.x updates. No subscriptions, no iLok, no dongle.
How is the web version different from the plugin?
The web version (demo.html) uses Web Audio API with single-precision float processing — great for auditioning the effect. The plugin version uses 64-bit double precision DSP, adds MIDI learn, A/B comparison, preset save/load, unlimited preset slots, and lower latency (26 samples vs browser latency). The fan DSP engine is the same in both — the same blade occlusion math, the same comb filter, the same motor hum.
