The Physics

How a box fan
makes you sound robotic.

Three physical mechanisms. One plugin. Zero psychoacoustic tricks.

1. Blade-Pass Amplitude Modulation Primary

As each of the three fan blades crosses the vocal path, it occludes the sound — fast cut-in (sharp blade edge) then slow release (rounded exit). The result: the voice is literally carved into pieces at 18-72 Hz. This is NOT amplitude modulation in the classic sine-wave sense. It is a gate. The voice disappears between blade passes. Listeners perceive pitch because the remaining fragments interpolate the missing fundamental (the "Auto-Tune before Auto-Tune" illusion).

2. Reflected Blade Path Dominant at Grille

The blade-reflected path is louder than the direct bone-conduction path when your lips are on the guard. At GRILLE position, only 5% of the signal comes through the direct path — the rest is blade-scattered, chopped, and doppler-shifted. At BEHIND, the direct path rises to 28%, softening the chop and adding the airy, roomy character of rear-intake fan singing.

3. Housing Comb & Motor Stack

The plastic fan housing creates a multi-tap comb filter (4-8 reflections, 0.4-3.5 ms delays) from sound bouncing between the grille and the rear housing. The induction motor injects a 60 Hz fundamental + 120 Hz harmonic with BPF-sideband modulation. At BEHIND position, these dominate the texture — the warm, humming, plastic-box character that defines the nostalgic rear-fan sound.

4. Perceptual Pitch from Chop

When the blade gate removes most of the vocal energy, the brain's auditory system fills in the missing pitch information from the remaining fragments. This creates the illusion of pitch correction — what YouTube users called "singing into a fan live auto-tune." FanTune's vintage pitch mode amplifies this by gating the grain shifter OFF during blade close instead of trying to pitch-correct silence.

FAQ

Common questions.

Is this just another Auto-Tune clone?
No. FanTune is the opposite of Auto-Tune. The primary effect is amplitude modulation from fan blades — the voice gets literally chopped into pieces. Pitch correction is a secondary layer that gates off during blade close. The "pitchy" sound comes from the chop itself, not from grain shifting. This is what people actually heard when they sang into fans in 2005, not what modern pitch correction sounds like.
What's the difference between GRILLE and BEHIND?
GRILLE (distance knob toward max) simulates lips pressed against the front guard. The blade-reflected path dominates (only 5% direct sound). AM depth reaches 30 dB. Duty cycle is ~40% closed — hard, aggressive chop. BEHIND (distance toward min) simulates 6-12" from the intake side. More direct path (28%). Softer chop (16 dB depth). Motor hum and turbulence increase. This is the nostalgic rear-fan sound that most people remember.
Does it work on instruments besides voice?
Yes. It was designed for vocals but the blade-pass modulation works on anything — guitars, synths, drums, full mixes. Acoustic instruments with natural transient content respond especially well because the blade gate interacts with their attack/release envelope. Try it on a clean electric guitar riff for instant bedroom-fan lo-fi.
What DAWs are supported?
Any DAW that supports VST3 (Ableton Live 11+, FL Studio, Cubase, Reaper, Studio One, Bitwig) or AU (Logic Pro, GarageBand). CLAP format is also supported for DAWs that support it (Bitwig, Reaper with CLAP). The standalone version works as a regular audio application with file playback.
Can I use the interactive demo for real production?
The interactive browser demo uses a Web Audio API approximation of the core fan DSP. It's great for auditioning the effect. For full-quality 64-bit processing, factory presets, preset saving, MIDI learn, and the complete feature set, download the plugin.
Is there an A/B comparison feature?
Yes. The plugin has a built-in A/B comparison slot. Click the A/B button in the header to toggle between two complete snapshots of every parameter. Additionally, the Mix knob lets you blend dry and wet signals, and the Bypass button (LED in the top-right) compares processed vs. unprocessed instantly.
